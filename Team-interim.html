Ahin Roy

Team Lead


                        

Ahin was born in Bishnupur, the temple town of West Bengal, India. After his initial training comprising of a memorable schooling at RKMV, Narendrapur & an undergraduate chemistry degree at RKMRC Narendrapur in University of Calcutta, he moved to IISc Bangalore to pursue an Integrated Ph. D degree in Chemical Sciences. This is where his inclination towards the interdisciplinary subject of materials science started, deviating from the popular career pathways in inorganic & organic chemistry. This resulted in a Ph. D degree from Materials Research Centre, with a thesis based on atomistic simulations of metal nanowires. Following this, Ahin moved to the Department of Materials Science & Engineering, Kyushu University, Japan as a JSPS Postdoctoral Fellow and had a rigorous training in aberration-corrected scanning transmission electron microscopy (AC-STEM) on various JEOL-ARM machines. After this, being trained in a two-pronged approach of simulation and experiments to interrogate materials at atomic-scale, he spent a brief stint at IISc Bangalore as a visiting scientist experimenting with an aberration-corrected FEI (now ThermoFisher) TITAN G2. This was followed by another postdoc tenure at Trinity College Dublin at Advanced Microscopy Laboratory, where he carried out extensive work with a Nion UltraSTEM200, a microscope which is a true milestone in spectrum imaging of materials. In 2022, Ahin started his own group at Materials Science Centre, IIT-KGP focusing on his combinatorial expertise.
